Guidelines for walk leaders and backmarkers

What do walk leaders need to do/carry with them?

  1. Read the walk leader’s checklist. Please print this and keep it in your folder.
  2. Recce the route before leading the walk.
  3. Carry a charged mobile phone and have the contact number of the back-marker. You might need this in case of an emergency, and also to communicate with the back-marker.
  4. Wear a high-viz vest (given once qualified as a walk leader).
  5. Complete a copy of the walk register, add names of all walkers and guests.
  6. Keep a spare copy of the walker registration form – to be completed by all new walkers; we need photo permissions too.
  7. Keep a printed copy of the accident / incident/ near misses report form. Read the Guidance document.
  8. Carry copies of the walks leaflet to hand to newcomers and people encountered on walks. Good if all our walkers are given a couple of copies, so they could take that with them when next they see their GP.
  9. Ask new walkers to add their ‘in case of an emergency’ contact as ICE on their mobile phone and/or to carry a completed ICE card in their pocket.
  10. Remember to mention at the start of every walk that walkers walk with us at their own risk, and to also notify with the front or back marker if you decide to leave the walk.
  11. Walkers are allowed to bring well-behaved dogs on short leads. Please remind dog walkers to walk at the back of the group.
  12. When crossing a road, remind all walkers to cross when they feel safe to do so, however, it is advised to all cross in one group; wait on the other side until the group is together again.
  13. The walk leader has to make sure the pace is right for the walk group by stopping and making eye contact with the back marker from time to time.
  14. Please email pictures to: Membership@Guildfordhealthwalks.org.uk
  15. Walk risk assessments need to be updated every year in April and dated.

Continuously do risk assessments whilst walking and note down any obstructions requiring changes to the walk route; update the risk assessment and send it to membership@Guildfordhealthwalks.org.uk

First Aid training is on offer to all volunteers; this is not obligatory. If you are interested, this can be arranged. Contact volunteers@Guildfordhealthwalks.org.uk
